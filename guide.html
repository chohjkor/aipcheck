Aging in Place Readiness Guide

Aging in Place refers to the ability of individuals to live safely, independently, and comfortably in their own homes as they grow older. Rather than relocating to institutional care facilities, many people prefer to remain in familiar environments that support their physical, emotional, and social well-being.

Why Aging in Place Matters

As people age, everyday environments can become challenging. Stairs, narrow doorways, poor lighting, and inaccessible bathrooms increase the risk of falls and injuries. Planning for Aging in Place helps reduce these risks and supports long-term independence.

Key Factors to Evaluate

  • Home accessibility (entrances, stairs, door widths)
  • Bathroom and kitchen safety
  • Proximity to healthcare and essential services
  • Community infrastructure and walkability
  • Ability to adapt the home over time

How AIP Check Helps

AIP Check is an independent informational tool designed to help individuals understand whether their current living environment is suitable for Aging in Place. It provides general guidance and educational insights to support informed decision-making.
